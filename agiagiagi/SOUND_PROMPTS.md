# AGI Sound Design — ElevenLabs Generation Prompts

Theme: Disco Elysium meets phone companion. Melancholic, analog, literary. Everything should feel like it belongs in a smoky detective's office that happens to exist inside a phone camera.

---

## BGM Loops

### 1. idle_ambient.mp3 — Camera Active, Nothing Happening

> Extremely soft ambient loop. A single detuned upright piano key pressed once every 4 seconds, each note swimming in deep plate reverb that decays slowly. Warm analog tape hiss underneath, barely perceptible. A distant, muffled church bell tolls once at the very end. The feeling of standing alone on a wet city bridge at 3am, watching your breath disappear. No drums, no melody, just texture and emptiness. Tempo: essentially none. Volume: whisper-quiet background. 12 seconds, seamless loop.

### 2. observing_drone.mp3 — AI Is Analyzing the Scene

> A warm analog synthesizer pad slowly inhaling — a single sustained chord that builds from silence over 4 seconds, holds for 4, then gently recedes. Think Juno-106 with the filter opening just slightly. Underneath: vinyl surface noise, the faintest crackle of a record between songs. One distant plucked nylon guitar string at the midpoint, a single note with long reverb tail, as if someone in another room touched an instrument by accident. The feeling of a thought forming before you have words for it. Contemplative, interior, like your brain humming while it works. 10 seconds, seamless loop.

### 3. artifact_reveal.mp3 — Artifact Card Slides Into View (one-shot)

> A cinematic micro-swell: warm French horn and muted trumpet play a single unresolved jazz chord, rising from nothing over 1.5 seconds, reaching a gentle peak, then dissolving into soft reverb tail over another 1.5 seconds. Mixed with the quiet sound of a heavy leather-bound book being opened on a wooden desk. The feeling of a case file being placed in front of you — something has been found. Not triumphant, more like quiet significance. Restrained, noir, momentous but intimate. 3 seconds total.

### 4. narration_bed.mp3 — Plays Under Chat Bubbles

> Ultra-quiet jazz texture loop. A brush lightly circling on a snare drum in no particular rhythm, just gentle shushing. A single upright bass note plucked once, deep and round, with natural room ambience. The faint clink of ice in a glass far away. Smoky late-night lounge atmosphere where the band stopped playing 20 minutes ago but the drummer is still absent-mindedly touching the kit. No melody, no progression, just atmosphere. The sonic equivalent of dim lighting. 8 seconds, seamless loop.

### 5. tension_ambient.mp3 — High Risk / Error State

> Low rumbling drone, like a building's HVAC system heard through concrete walls. A single cello note bowed extremely slowly with heavy rosin pressure, creating an uneasy sustained tone. Occasional faint metallic ping, like a radiator cooling in an empty hallway. The feeling of something being slightly wrong but you can't identify what. Unsettling but not aggressive — more existential dread than danger. No sharp sounds, everything padded and muffled. 10 seconds, seamless loop.

## UI Sound Effects

### 6. bubble_appear.mp3 — Companion Chat Bubble Appears

> A single vintage mechanical typewriter key being struck — the full sound: the clack of the key lever, the tiny thud of the typebar hitting the platen through an ink ribbon, and the faint ring of the mechanism settling. Recorded close-mic on a 1960s Olympia typewriter. Add gentle plate reverb (medium decay, 0.8 seconds) to give it warmth and space, as if the typewriter sits in a large quiet room. The sound should feel tactile and satisfying — a thought being committed to paper. 0.5 seconds total.

### 7. chip_tap.mp3 — User Taps a Suggestion Chip

> The sound of a fingertip tapping once on a crystal whiskey tumbler — a clean, bright, glassy 'tink' with a short pure resonance that fades over 0.3 seconds. Not a wine glass (too high), not a beer glass (too dull) — specifically the thick-walled clarity of cut crystal. Add a tiny amount of warm room reverb. The feeling of making a decisive small choice — like checking a box on a detective's evidence board. Clean, satisfying, precise. 0.4 seconds total.

### 8. artifact_open.mp3 — Artifact Overlay Slides Up

> The layered sound of: (1) a manila folder being slid across a wooden desk surface, creating a soft papery friction sound over 0.8 seconds, (2) mixed with a subtle synthesizer shimmer — a high-passed sparkle that rises gently, like a soft digital chime made of glass particles. The paper-meets-digital fusion. The feeling of a case file being opened and light catching the photos inside. Not flashy, more like controlled revelation. 1.5 seconds total.

### 9. artifact_close.mp3 — Artifact Dismissed

> A hardcover book being closed with one hand — the soft 'whump' of pages compressing together followed by the quiet thud of covers meeting. Medium-sized book, not a slam, just a purposeful close. Recorded on a wooden surface. Short reverb tail in a quiet room. The feeling of finishing a chapter — not sad, just complete. A period at the end of a sentence. 0.6 seconds total.

### 10. mode_switch.mp3 — Toggle Between Companion and Crowd Notes Mode

> An old analog radio tuning dial being turned one click — a brief burst of warm static (0.3 seconds), the ghost of a distant voice caught between stations for a split second, then settling into clear silence with a soft 'click' as the dial locks into position. Think 1970s shortwave radio, Bakelite knob, warm and analog. The feeling of shifting your attention from one frequency of reality to another. 1 second total.

### 11. error_sound.mp3 — Something Went Wrong

> A single muted trombone note, played with a plunger mute — the classic 'wah' sound but performed very quietly and cut short, as if the musician started to play a sad note and immediately thought better of it. Self-aware and slightly comedic, like the narrator acknowledging a fumble with wry acceptance. Not alarming, more like a gentle shrug expressed as sound. Add light room reverb. 0.7 seconds total.

### 12. object_detected.mp3 — New Object Found in Crowd Notes

> A vintage Polaroid camera shutter firing — the distinctive mechanical 'ka-chunk' of the mirror flipping and the shutter blade opening and closing, followed by the quiet whir of the motor beginning to eject the film. Specifically the SX-70 model's softer, more refined mechanism, not the harsh snap of cheaper models. The feeling of capturing a moment — freezing something in place for examination. 0.8 seconds total.

### 13. pin_note.mp3 — User Pins a Crowd Note

> A steel map pin being pressed into a cork board — the brief friction of the pin point entering cork, ending with a tiny satisfying 'tck' as it seats firmly. Very small, very precise. Like the sound you'd hear if you leaned close to a detective's evidence board as they pinned a new photo to it. Almost ASMR-level intimacy. 0.3 seconds total.

### 14. thinking_tick.mp3 — AI Processing Indicator

> A single tick of a mechanical wall clock — specifically the escapement mechanism of a pendulum clock, with the metallic 'tick' followed by the tiniest resonance of the clock case. Not a wristwatch (too thin), not a grandfather clock (too deep) — a medium wall clock in a quiet office. The feeling of time being measured while someone thinks. 0.2 seconds total.

### 15. success_chime.mp3 — Action Completed Successfully

> Two soft vibraphone notes played in quick succession — a rising minor third interval (like D4 to F4), each note struck gently with soft mallets. The bars ring with their characteristic warm shimmer, decaying naturally over 1.5 seconds. Not bright or celebratory — more like a quiet acknowledgment, the way a wise person nods when something clicks into place. Warm and slightly melancholic even in success. 1.5 seconds total.

### 16. scroll_past.mp3 — Scrolling Through Conversation History

> A single page of thin paper being turned — the soft 'fwip' of the page lifting and settling, like turning a page in a well-worn paperback novel. Very quiet, very fast. The briefest whisper of paper moving through air. 0.2 seconds total.

## Narrator Voice Profile (ElevenLabs Conversational Agent)

### Voice Description
> Male, mid-40s, world-weary but endlessly curious. Speaks in a measured cadence with deliberate pauses between phrases, as if weighing each word before releasing it. Slight natural rasp from years of late nights. A voice that has read too many case files and too much poetry and can no longer tell the difference. Dry humor delivered completely deadpan — the jokes land three seconds after he's moved on. Baritone range, never raises his voice. The vocal equivalent of a worn leather armchair in a room full of books. Optional: very slight Eastern European inflection, just enough to suggest the words were originally thought in another language.

### Sample Lines for Voice Cloning / Testing
- "That keyboard has seen things. The shine on WASD tells a story the owner won't."
- "A thermostat set to twenty-two. Someone here knows exactly what they want from the universe."
- "The cable management tells me everything. This is a person who has given up on certain battles and won others."
- "Interesting. The dust pattern suggests this was moved recently. Not cleaned — moved. There's a difference."
- "I've seen enough notebooks to know: the ones with dot grids belong to people who believe in systems. Whether the systems work is another matter entirely."

## Future: Disco Elysium Stat System

Give users a stat profile (similar to DE's 24 skills) that changes how the companion perceives and narrates. Stats could be set during onboarding or evolve based on which chips the user taps over time.

### Proposed Stats (adapted for a camera companion)

| Stat | DE Equivalent | Effect on Companion |
|------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Inland Empire** | Inland Empire | High: companion talks TO objects, gives them personalities and inner lives. "The stapler is tired. It has held too many things together." Unlocks surreal object dialogues. |
| **Encyclopedia** | Encyclopedia | High: companion drops deep factual knowledge — manufacturing dates, material science, historical context. "That's a Cherry MX Blue switch. Patented 1983. The click is intentional." |
| **Empathy** | Empathy | High: companion reads the human story behind objects — wear patterns, personal habits, emotional traces. "Someone grips this mug with both hands. They drink slowly. They're thinking." |
| **Visual Calculus** | Visual Calculus | High: companion notices spatial relationships, angles, symmetry, structural details. Generates layout diagrams, spatial analyses. |
| **Electrochemistry** | Electrochemistry | High: companion is drawn to sensory details — textures, colors, smells it imagines. More poetic, more visceral. "That leather is crying out to be touched." |
| **Rhetoric** | Rhetoric | High: companion argues with itself, presents multiple interpretations, plays devil's advocate. "On one hand... but consider..." |
| **Shivers** | Shivers | High: companion gets flashes about the broader context — the neighborhood, the city, the time of day. Ambient awareness beyond the frame. |
| **Conceptualization** | Conceptualization | High: companion sees everything as art/design. Critiques composition, suggests reframing, references art movements. |

### Implementation Notes
- Could replace or augment the existing `expertise` and `outputStyle` profile fields
- Stats affect the system prompt dynamically — high Inland Empire injects "You may address objects directly as characters"
- Stats could be 1-6 scale (like DE) or simpler 1-3 (low/medium/high)
- Learning system already tracks practical vs creative weight — stats could be a richer version of this
- Killer feature: when Inland Empire is maxed, tapping an object in Crowd Notes opens a dialogue WITH the object ("The keyboard speaks: 'They never clean between my keys. I remember every crumb.'")
